How Does Hydro-electric Energy Work?

Hydro-electic energy is the process of using the natural flow of water to produce energy. It can come in all shapes and sizes, from massive dams to water wheels you may see by the side of a pond. How do these methods turn the flow of water of water into energy? Flowing water turns a wheel, and the energy from spinning the wheel is then converted into energy. Dams work by funneling the water into the path of a wheel and then funneling the water out the other side. Hydropower accounts for 66% of Washington's net energy today.

Pros

  • Hydro-electric is a clean form of energy, releasing little to no pollution and CO2.
  • Hydro-electric energy is renewable and will not run out as long as there is a source of flowing water.
  • Enjoys low cost of maintenence once it is set up.

    • Cons

  • Requires a body of flowing water to be able to harness so it isn't readily avaliable everywhere.
  • Large upfront cost to build a dam.
  • Dams can damage the wildlife and ecosystems around it.
